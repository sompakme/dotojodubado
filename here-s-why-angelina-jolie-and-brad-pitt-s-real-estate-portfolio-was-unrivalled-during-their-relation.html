Inside Brad Pitt And Angelina Jolie's French Quarter Mansion

This 7,000-square-foot, five-bedroom, five-bathroom mansion (which also contains a two-story guest house) was purchased by Angelina Jolie and Brad Pitt in late 2006 while the actor was in town filming The Curious Case of Benjamin Button.

The home's interior boasts a mix of old and new, with exquisite touches like Venetian plaster walls, a spiral staircase, and marble mantels next to modern conveniences like an elevator. The main house of this quaint residence has four bedrooms, four full bathrooms, and two half bathrooms, as the Los Angeles Times reported.

Despite constructing a guesthouse that doubled the number of bedrooms to five, the house was still relatively small for the well-known family of eight. However, the Jolie-Pitts were excellent hosts, having welcomed celebrity pals like Jonah Hill, who stayed at the mansion for a month while filming 21 Jump Street. Fellow actor Matthew McConaughey also has a house directly across the street.

In 2016, the celebrity couple who had initially paid $3.5 million for the estate built in the 1830s sold it for $4.9 million. This deal closed a little over a month after Jolie's September 2016 divorce filing, in which she cited "irreconcilable differences" as the basis for ending the marriage.

How Much Did Brad Pitt And Angelina Jolie Spend On Château Miraval?

In August 2014, Pitt and Jolie tied the knot in a secret ceremony at the Romanesque chapel of Château Miraval, one of the most spectacular estates the pair has purchased together. The couple rented the building in 2008 for a total of three years before purchasing it for $60 million (£52.7m) in 2012.

The expansive property comprises a total area of one thousand acres and several buildings dating back to the 17th century. In addition, there is a lake, 20 different fountains, and a vineyard actively being farmed. It is said that Pitt has left his mark on the house, which includes 35 bedrooms, a swimming pool, a billiards room, a screening room, twin gyms, a sauna, a Jacuzzi, and a banquet hall. In addition, the property has a screening room.

The pair continued the family heritage of making wine, thereby starting Miraval Wines in 2013. When Pitt and Jolie split up after an incident on their private jet, Pitt continued to be heavily involved with the company, creating his Fleur de Miraval rosé champagne, which was shared at the Oscars in 2022.
