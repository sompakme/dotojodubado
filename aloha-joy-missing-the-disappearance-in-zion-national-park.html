Aloha Joy Missing: The Disappearance In Zion National Park

August 06, 2024
blog

The news of Aloha Joy Missing while hiking The Narrows at Zion National Park spread quickly on social media.

Aloha Joy, a 14-year-old, went missing while on a family hike in The Narrows at Zion National Park. 

She was separated from the group after she tried to catch up with her faster relatives and mistakenly turned into Orderville Canyon instead. 

Aloha Joy was reported missing, and a search operation led by Zion National Park crews ensued.

Rescue after an overnight stranded in the narrows

Fortunately, Aloha Joy was rescued the following morning after she spent an entire night alone and hungry in the canyon. 

The teenager had bunkered down in the canyon after losing her shoes while trying to climb a huge rock. She resorted to screaming for help but, to no avail, spent the night alone and cold.

The delay in realizing that Aloha Joy was missing heightened the family’s concern. Aloha’s mother, Aurelia Lunceford, expressed how she felt when Aloha’s absence was discovered. 

They had all assumed that Aloha was with either the faster or slower group, who were trekking the canyon. 

She admitted that she was terrified when both parties returned without her daughter.

Moreover, Aurelia was worried for Aloha, especially since the latter did not have any food or water with her. 

Aloha was not wearing appropriate clothing to protect against the cold in the canyon. 

Also, Aurelia admitted that overtaking the fear of losing her child was the most challenging part of the ordeal.

Aloha’s attempt to rejoin the group

Aloha Joy’s wrong turn into Orderville Canyon was accidental, as she was following a person she thought was her brother. 

She thought it would be easier to rejoin the group by doing so. 

However, the farther she went, the more challenging the hike became. She eventually realized that she was alone and lost in the canyon.

Aurelia revealed that Aloha had made some attempts to climb a huge rock after losing her shoes. 

Her knees ended up bruised, and she was unable to proceed further. She spent the night there, cold and wracked with fear. 

Nevertheless, Aloha remained calm and resolute; she wore her emergency blanket and waited for a rescue team. She also knew it was best to keep warm and stay put while she waited.

Rescue team’s heroic efforts

After Aloha Joy was reported missing and all indications pointed to her being lost in the canyon, the National Park Service could only conduct a search operation the next day. 

They dispatched two teams that entered The Narrows early in the morning. 

The Search operation was not easy as the terrain at Zion National Park is rocky and difficult to navigate. 

They were guided by the reports that came in from those who had seen Aloha Joy missing poster on social media.

The search team was focused on Orderville Canyon, where they eventually found Aloha Joy. 

After an overnight ordeal, Aloha Joy Missing was rescued by Zion National Park crews the following morning.

They were thrilled to have located her and safely transported her out of the canyon.

A reunion with Aloha Joy

The Lunceford family was thrilled to have Aloha Joy back in their midst. 

Aurelia expressed her gratitude to the rescue team and everyone whose concern and support had brought Aloha home safely. 

Lunceford wrote on a social media post: “We can’t even begin to express how grateful we are to everyone who helped get my baby girl back home safe and sound!”.

On their part, Zion National Park management expressed their relief that Aloha Joy had been returned safely. 

They reiterated their warning to hikers to follow safety guidelines and seek help in case of emergencies.

Lessons to learn from Aloha Joy’s ordeal

Aloha Joy’s ordeal highlights the dangers of undertaking hikes without proper preparations. One can easily get lost in the canyon’s rocky terrain and harsh weather conditions. 

One of the lessons we should learn as hikers is to have a well-thought-out plan before embarking on hikes. 

Always carry proper gear and essentials such as food, water, first aid kits, and emergency blankets.

It is essential to adhere to safety guidelines in National Parks, maintain constant communication with other hikers, and always follow the designated routes. 

In case of an emergency, hikers should seek help promptly and adhere to the advice given by search teams.

Conclusion

Aloha Joy’s Ordeal in Zion National Park Narrows highlights the importance of proper hiking preparations. 

A wrong turn into Orderville Canyon led to a night-long ordeal that saw her stranded alone and freezing. 

It took the concerted efforts of the rescue team and those who followed up on the social media alerts to locate and bring her home safely. 

It is important to adhere to safety guidelines and always be vigilant in the canyon to avoid a similar incident.
