Dak Prescott Biography

Dak Prescott is an American football quarterback for the Dallas Cowboys of the National Football League (NFL).

Dak Prescott Age

He was born on 29 July 1993 in Sulphur, Louisiana, United States. He celebrates his birthday on 29 July every year.

Dak Prescott College

He studied Haughton High School in Haughton, Louisiana, and joined Mississippi State University.

Dak Prescott NFL

He began his career in high school, where he played football for the Buccaneers. He led Haughton to become 2010 District 1-AAAA Champions completing with 951 yards on 90 attempts with 17 touchdowns. He completed 159-of-258 passes for 2,860 yards and 39 touchdowns as a junior.

In college, in 2011, he was redshirted as a true freshman with Mississippi State. He led the Bulldogs to its first #1 ranking in program history, with a 10-2 regular-season record, and led them to the Orange Bowl in his first season as a full-time starter in 2014.

He was named by SI.com, a 2014 Honorable Mention All-American, he was also named to the 2014 First-Team All-SEC team by the Coaches, AP, and ESPN.com, he was on the 2014 SEC Fall Academic Honor Roll. He won the Conerly Trophy, was a finalist for the Maxwell Award, the Johnny Unitas Golden Arm Award, the Davey O’Brien award, and the Manning Award.

In the 2015 season, he was named a 2015 National Player of the Year Candidate, was chosen First Team Preseason All-SEC by the media and was selected to two Preseason All-American teams. He was the fourth player in FBS history to pass for 60 touchdowns and rush for 40 touchdowns in a career, he joined Dan LeFevour of Tim Tebow of Florida, Central Michigan, and Colin Kaepernick of Nevada. He finished fourth in total touchdowns and third in SEC history in total yards. He holds 38 school records.

In the 2016 season, Rookie year, he was selected by the Dallas Cowboys in the fourth round of the 2016 NFL Draft. He was named the NFL Offensive Rookie of the Year at the end of the season, receiving 28 of 50 votes, beating out running back and teammate Ezekiel Elliott by 7 votes. His peers ranked him 14th on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2017. In 2017 season, he finished his second professional season with 3,324 passing yards, 22 passing touchdowns, 357 rushing yards, 13 interceptions, and six rushing touchdowns.
