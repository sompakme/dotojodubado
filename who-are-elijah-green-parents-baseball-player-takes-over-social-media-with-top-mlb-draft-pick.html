Meet Elijah Green Dad Eric And Mom Leslie Green

Elijah Green’s dad Eric Green was a two-time Pro Bowler and a 1990 All-Pro tight end with the Steelers, Dolphins, Ravens, and Jets during his 10-year NFL career.

The Pittsburgh Steelers selected Green twenty-first overall in the NFL Draft. He first went on strike for 54 days, missing training camp, preseason, and the first game of the 1990 NFL season.

Green initially threatened to sit out the full season, which would have qualified him for the 1991 NFL Draft.

His arrival at Steelers caused a revolution in the game. Until Green returned to the lineup, the Steeler’s offense failed to score a touchdown.

He caught five of Bubby Brister’s seven touchdown throws when he returned. As a result of Green’s comeback, defensive line coach Joe Greene stated, “Without Eric Green, we would not have scored a touchdown last year.”

Green’s flexibility was demonstrated in his first season when he was asked to play wide receiver, slotback, wingback, H-back, and even running back.

Green signed a six-year, $12 million contract with the Miami Dolphins on March 11, 1995, becoming the highest-paid tight end in NFL history at the time of his signing.

Unfortunately, Green was released by the Miami Dolphins on July 9, 1996, by then-coach Jimmy Johnson due to his weight issues and work ethic.

In late September 1996, he worked out with the Ravens before finishing his NFL career with the New York Jets.

Once resigned from the NFL, he moved to Florida with his wife Leslie Green, and two children. He is the father of three sons and four daughters.
