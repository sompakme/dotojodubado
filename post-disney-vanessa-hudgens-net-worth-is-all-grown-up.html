Vanessa Hudgens' Film And Television Career

Vanessa Hudgens' career began with minor screen appearances.

Her first credited role came in an episode of Still Standing in 2002, followed by appearances on shows like Robbery Homicide Division.

She made her film debut in the 2003 drama Thirteen, before landing the role that would catapult her to fame – Gabriella Montez in Disney's High School Musical.

This smash hit, co-starring Zac Efron, drew in a record-breaking 7.7 million viewers on its premiere night.

The sequel, High School Musical 2, premiered in 2007 to an even larger audience, becoming the most-watched Disney Channel Original Movie ever — with over 17.2 million viewers.

Hudgens reprised her role once more in the theatrical release High School Musical 3: Senior Year. This film opened at number one at the box office and ultimately grossed $252 million worldwide.

Following High School Musical, Hudgens ventured into a variety of films. She showcased her musical talents in Bandslam, and her dramatic range in Beastly.

She also starred in action films like Sucker Punch and Journey 2: The Mysterious Island, which grossed a global total of $335 million.

More recent years have seen Hudgens take on roles in romantic comedies like Second Act, and holiday films like The Knight Before Christmas, which she also co-produced. The Daily Beast dubbed Hudgens "Netflix's Christmas Queen":

"[In November 2021], Netflix’s unrivaled Queen of Christmas, Vanessa Hudgens, is donning her tiara once again in the third installment of the Princess Switch series.

"I’m just going to go ahead and answer the question on everybody’s mind right off the bat: No, Vanessa Hudgens does not play a fourth character in The Princess Switch 3: Romancing the Star."

In 2020, Hudgens appeared in the third entry in the Bad Boys franchise, Bad Boys For Life, which grossed over $426 million worldwide.

Hudgens has also appeared in many television productions. In 2017, she played the lead role in the first and only season of the NBC series Powerless.

Vanessa Is Also A Musician

The High School Musical franchise helped Vanessa Hudgens to stardom, launching both her acting and musical careers.

The soundtracks to these films highlighted her vocal talents, and hit songs – like "Breaking Free," her duet with Zac Efron – reached the top five on the Billboard Hot 100 charts.

Capitalizing on this momentum, in 2006, Hudgens released her debut solo album, V. It debuted at a respectable number 24 on the Billboard 200 chart, selling over 34,000 copies in its first week.

The album ultimately sold 570,000 copies in the US alone, later achieving a Gold certification from the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).

Her second album Identified followed in 2008, but despite reaching the Billboard 200, it marked a decline in sales — which led to Hudgens parting ways with her record label, Hollywood Records.

After the High School Musical series, Hudgens took a break from her musical career to focus on acting; nevertheless, her earlier releases undoubtedly contributed to her overall net worth.

Vanessa's Business Ventures

Vanessa Hudgens is multi-hyphenate, both on screen and in the business world.

What Is A "Multi-Hyphenate"? Particularly in the entertainment industry, a "multi-hyphenate" is a person who not only has myriad skills and descriptors (such as actor and singer), but also is successful in diverse aspects of the field.

Vanessa has co-founded two businesses, demonstrating her innovation and expanding her income stream beyond traditional entertainment.

One such venture is Caliwater, a beverage company co-founded with Oliver Trevena. This line of cactus water caters to the health-conscious market, offering a refreshing and hydrating drink rich in antioxidants.

Caliwater taps into the growing consumer demand for functional beverages, positioning itself as a healthy alternative to sugary drinks.

In 2021, she co-founded Know Beauty, a skincare line launched alongside singer Madison Beer. This venture caters to a different market but demonstrates Hudgens' interest in the beauty and wellness industry.

Know Beauty underwent a re-branding in 2023, aiming to expand its reach to generate more revenues, and in September, Hudgens became the brand's sole CEO.

Vanessa Hudgens' Real Estate Portfolio

In 2008, Hudgens paid $2.75 million for a 5,200 square-foot home in Studio City, California for $2.75 million.

This spacious residence boasted six bedrooms, a large living room opening onto a terrace, a gourmet kitchen, and luxurious amenities like a wine cellar and a swimming pool.

After listing it for $3.85 million in 2019, she finally sold it for $3.15 million, sums which likely comprised a large portion of Vanessa Hudgens' net worth.

A decade later, the Second Act actress upgraded to a Los Feliz home bought from actor Gary Oldman for $4.9 million.

Hudgens made a tidy profit in 2022 when she sold this property to Ronan Farrow and Jon Lovett for $6.7 million. Her real estate portfolio also includes a $1 million condo in Brooklyn, New York.
