Steel Panther, originally known as Metal Skool, was formed in 2000. They gained popularity by performing at the Viper Room, a renowned venue in Los Angeles. Their energetic and humorous performances quickly attracted a dedicated fan base, and they soon became a staple of the local rock scene.

In 2003, the band released their debut EP titled “Hole Patrol,” showcasing their unique blend of glam metal and comedic lyricism. This marked the beginning of their journey as a recording artist and laid the foundation for their future success.

In 2008, Metal Skool underwent a significant transformation and changed their name to Steel Panther, a moniker that would better reflect their evolving sound and identity. With their newfound name, they signed a record deal with Republic Records and embarked on a journey that would solidify their place in the music industry.
