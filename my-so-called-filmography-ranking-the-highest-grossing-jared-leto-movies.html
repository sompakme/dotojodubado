10 Urban Legend (1998)

Global Box Office, Urban Legend - $72,527,595

Before Jared Leto delved into the dark psyche of comic book villains and enigmatic cult leaders, he dipped his toes in slasher films like 1998's Urban Legend.

This teen scream flick, directed by Jamie Blanks, throws audiences into a whirlwind of suspense as a series of murders on a New England college campus unfold, each inspired by popular urban legends.

Leto portrays Paul Gardener, a charming yet mysterious college student with a knack for horror trivia. Urban Legend received generally negative reviews from critics, with criticism being that the film was a blatant rip-off of Scream.

9 The Thin Red Line (1998)

Global Box Office, The Thin Red Line - $98,126,565

Terrence Malick's poignant masterpiece, The Thin Red Line, transports us to the brutal realities of the Guadalcanal Campaign in World War II.

This hauntingly beautiful war epic follows the soldiers as they navigate the emotional and physical devastation of combat. Jared Leto portrayed Lieutenant William Whyte in the film.

Though his screen time was limited, Leto's performance as a young soldier struggling to reconcile the idealism he brought to war with the horrors he witnessed on the battlefield was compelling.

The Thin Red Line earned widespread critical acclaim for its unflinching portrayal of war, and its exploration of the human cost of conflict.

The film garnered seven Academy Award nominations, including nods for "Best Picture" and "Best Director."

8 Fight Club (1999)

Global Box Office, Fight Club - $101,209,702

David Fincher's iconic cult classic Fight Club may have featured Jared Leto in a supporting role, but he stole the scene every chance he got.

Leto played Angel Face, a charismatic and enigmatic member of the titular underground fight club. Though his screen time was limited, his performance oozes eccentricity and menace, perfectly complementing the film's exploration of societal disillusionment and rebellion.

Despite critical acclaim and its status as a cultural touchstone, the film underperformed at the box office, grossing $37 million domestically and $63.8 million internationally, for a total of $100.9 million.

Subsequent re-releases pushed the final figure to $101.2 million, a respectable sum considering its modest $63 million production budget.

Global Box Office, Haunted Mansion - $117,449,790

Disney's spooky reboot of Haunted Mansion features Jared Leto in a chilling (albeit disembodied) role, in the second theme park ride to the screen adaptation of the iconic Disney World attraction.

Voicing the Hatbox Ghost, Alistair Crump, Leto adds his signature creepiness to the film's ghostly ensemble.

The story follows Gabbie and Travis, a widow and her son, who purchase a seemingly ordinary mansion only to discover it is teeming with spectral residents.

The film chronicles the rise and fall of the Gucci family empire, focusing on the events leading up to the assassination of Maurizio Gucci, heir to the Gucci fashion house.

Leto's portrayal of Paolo, an eccentric and often overlooked member of the family, stands out amid the star-studded cast:

4 Alexander (2004)

Global Box Office, Alexander - $167,298,192

Jared Leto sheds his contemporary persona to embody Hephaestion, the close confidante and rumored lover of Alexander the Great, in Oliver Stone's epic historical drama Alexander.

The film chronicles the legendary emperor's meteoric rise to power, his groundbreaking military conquests, and the emotional turmoil that shadowed his personal life.

Despite its grand cinematic scope and impressive production value, Alexander received mixed reviews, with some praising its visual grandeur and performances, while others criticized its historical inaccuracies and pacing.

Alexander earned $13.7 million in its opening weekend. At the end of its theatrical run, the film grossed $34.3 million in North America and $133 million overseas for a total of $167.3 million worldwide.

Considering its production budget of $155 million and other additional marketing costs, the film was a box-office bomb, with projected losses as high as $71 million.
