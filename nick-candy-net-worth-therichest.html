British luxury property developer Nick Candy has an estimated net worth of $1.1 billion. He earned his net worth as the co-founder of Candy & Candy, along with his brother, Christian. The company, founded in 1999 in Epsom, Surrey, specializes in interior home design and luxury real estate development worldwide. He now serves as its Chief Executive Officer.

Nick is a graduate of the University of Reading with a degree in Human Geography. His brother earned his business management degree at King’s College London. Together, they bought their first property in 1995, a one-bedroom flat in Radcliffe Square, Earls Court, London. With $9300 loan from their grandmother, they renovated their $190,000 apartment, selling it for $270,000 and making a profit of $80,000.

Between 1995 and 1999, the Candy brothers have renovated flats during their free time, which led to their property development business. They soon decided to leave their day jobs, and launched Candy & Candy in 1999. Their development projects, mostly in London, included the One Hyde Park apartment complex, NoHo Square and the Beverly Hills 9900 Wilshire development.
