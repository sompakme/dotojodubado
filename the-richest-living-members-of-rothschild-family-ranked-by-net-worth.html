The Richest Living Members Of Rothschild Family, Ranked By Net Worth

Highlights

  • The Rothschild Foundation acquired a lost masterpiece by Baroque painter Guercino in early 2024. The painting, depicting Moses, will go on public display at Waddesdon Manor in England.
  • The Rothschild family's wealth allows them to make expensive art acquisitions for their charitable foundation.
  • The Rothschild family's net worth is estimated to stand between $400 billion to $1 trillion, cumulatively.

The Rothschild name is connected with immense wealth, power, and prestige. In fact, the Rothschilds are a unique family which has accumulated vast riches over the last three centuries.

This multinational dynasty amassed billions through banking, growing from modest beginnings in Frankfurt, Germany in the 1700s with the founding of Mayer Amschel Rothschild to become one of the world's wealthiest families.

Updated, February 2024: On January 30th, artnet.com reported that the Rothschild Foundation had acquired a previously "lost" piece by Baroque painter Guernico (Giovanni Francesco Barbieri), and that the painting was to go on public display in England:

"A recently rediscovered masterpiece by the Baroque painter Guercino made headlines in 2022 after it overshot its $6,000 estimate to sell for a whopping $600,000 at auction in Paris.

"The dramatic portrayal of Moses looking up to the heavens with his palms raised will now go on public display at Waddesdon Manor in England, having [recently been] acquired by the Rothschild Foundation."

On January 30th, The Art Newspaper also covered the Rothschild Foundation's acquisition of the work, explaining its history as a once-lost work of art, and reporting:

"The whereabouts of Moses from the 1790s until the 2022 auction remain a mystery, and the seller’s name has not been disclosed. Until then, the composition was known only from three 17th-century painted copies, a drawing and engravings. These images show that it was always regarded as a very important Guercino."

The Art Newspaper further explained that the Rothschild family's moneyed charity facilitated the expensive painting's acquisition:

"Guercino’s Moses (around 1618-19) has been bought by Jacob Rothschild’s charitable foundation for around €2m. The painting is expected to be on permanent display at Waddesdon Manor, in Buckinghamshire, which is managed by the Rothschild Foundation on behalf of the National Trust.

"Few institutions in the UK are able to buy Old Master paintings at this sort of a price, but the foundation is backed by the wealth of the banking family. The charity’s income in the latest financial year was £41m."

She began her career by modeling for the painter Jean-Gabriel Domergue.

Her first role was in the 1949 short Un Chien et Madame. Rothschild subsequently starred in films such as The Sleepwalker, Le Passage de Venus, and others.

7 Édouard de Rothschild

Édouard de Rothschild's Net Worth (Estimated): $300 Million

Édouard de Rothschild has a $300 million fortune. Guy de Rothschild, the former head of the Rothschild & Cie bank, is his father.

In January 2005, Édouard de Rothschild committed to acquire a controlling stake of 37 percent in the French daily Libération.

In 2010, he became president of Libération's board and relocated to Israel.

When Patrick Drahi became the new owner of the newspaper in 2014, his stake was severely diminished.

She is now the CEO of E.L. Rothschild, a holding firm, and in a good position to remark on capitalism and philanthropy.

Nathaniel Rothschild's Net Worth (Estimated): $1 Billion

The New York Times has dubbed him "the man who may become the richest Rothschild":

Nathaniel 'Nat' Rothschild, who is just 50 years old, is the chosen successor to the title of Baron Rothschild, which is now held by his father, Jacob.

Nathaniel, a graduate of Eton College and Oxford University, has capitalized on his family's experience and established a vast array of commercial interests, having worked for hedge funds and invested substantially in coal mining around the world.

4 James Rothschild

James Rothschild's Net Worth (Estimated): $1.3 Billion

James Rothschild, a financier and banking heir, is another immensely wealthy member of the Rothschild family.

The British entrepreneur works for one of the world's major banking institutions, Rothschild Asset Management.

James is also famous for his marriage to the similarly wealthy heiress and socialite Nicky Hilton, which took place in 2015 at Kensington Palace, London – with Nicky's sister Paris Hilton serving as the maid of honor.

3 Ariane de Rothschild

Ariane de Rothschild's Net Worth (Estimated): $1.5 Billion

Ariane de Rothschild – the widow of the late Benjamin de Rothschild, a French banker, and owner of multiple vineyards – inherited her husband's immense fortune, which was valued at approximately $1.5 billion at the time of his death.

Benjamin was one of the wealthiest members of the Rothschild family at the time of his death.

He was the son of Edmond Adolphe, a financier, and head of the French branch of the family, and he inherited and expanded a vast family fortune.

He wed Ariane in 1999, and the couple has four daughters. She is the first woman to lead a Rothschild banking institution.

2 Jacob Rothschild

Jacob Rothschild's Net Worth (Estimated): $5 Billion

It is estimated that Jacob Rothschild, the 4th Baron Rothschild, is worth $5 billion; he is the father of the similarly affluent Nathaniel.

Jacob Rothschild is a British peer and investment banker who has worked for a number of London banks and has investments in investment trusts, wealth management enterprises, and in oil and gas.

He is also a notable philanthropist and major patron of the arts in the United Kingdom, for which he has received a multitude of distinguished accolades and awards.
