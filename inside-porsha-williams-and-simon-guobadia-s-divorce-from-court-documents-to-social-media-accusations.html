Porsha Williams Filed For Divorce From Simon Guobadia Because Of His Criminal Past

On February 22, Porsha Williams filed for divorce from Simon Guobadia after 15 months of marriage. According to court documents obtained by RadarOnline, Porsha admitted that reports of Simon's criminal history and the possibility of his deportation played a significant role in her decision to file for divorce. On January 11, 2024, Simon filed a case against the Director of Citizenship and Immigration Services after he requested citizenship and naturalization in July 2022, but his application was denied.

Guobadia's filing opened a can of worms that exposed his checkered past. The Nigerian native first entered the US through a Visitor's Visa in 1982 but overstayed. He applied for citizenship but was denied and deported. Guobadia managed to re-enter the country in 1986 and overstayed again. During that time, Simon was involved in criminal activities, including illegal vehicle use in 1989 and bank and credit card fraud in 1987, 1990, and 1991.

Simon also engaged in identity theft and used multiple aliases to apply for temporary resident status under the Social Agricultural Worker program. According to reports, Simon's new identity of Guobadia got him permanent residency in 1992, only 26 days after being deported for overstaying his 1986 6-month Visa. With Simon's fake identity coming to light, Porsha felt it would be safer for her to cut ties with her husband as he had become like a 'stranger' to her.

Porsha Williams And Simon Guobadia Have Been Embroiled In A Legal Battle Since Divorce Filing

Since Porsha filed for divorce from Simon the two have been engaged in heated back and forth that played out in court documents. The Real Housewives of Atlanta star demanded a judge enforce the prenup they had both signed so that Simon would leave the mansion. According to Porsha, the legally binding document states that in the event of divorce, the husband would have to vacate the home within 30 days of filing.

Guobadia allegedly requested an extension that would allow him to stay in his home until March 31, but Porsha refused it. On March 26, Porsha demanded an emergency hearing after Guobadia allegedly changed the locks and garage code of the mansion, thereby preventing her entry. In another court filing, Simon alleged that Porsha had voluntarily left their home and his children and returned with a gunman to pick up her belongings.

Simon allegedly had to call the police on Porsha on March 21 to maintain the peace in his home. Three days later, Guobadia alleges, Williams returned to his home with his mother and her mother’s boyfriend where they disengaged security cameras and tampered with evidence. After Simon’s claims, Porsha made shocking allegations that her ex was trying to ruin her reputation and that Simon was playing with the truth by trying to say the encounter with her security was ‘hostile’.

In response, Porsha filed court documents accusing her ex of bringing three women to their home and cheating on her. Porsha’s lawyer, Randall M. Kessler stated,

“Wife has returned to the residence on various occasions to retrieve various personal items belonging to Wife and her minor child, as well as to secure various items, after learning that Husband had at least three women in the marital residence on different evenings.”

Simon went on to plead for a Georgia judge to grant him a restraining order against Porsha accusing her of vilifying, threatening, molesting and harassing him, his children and his staff in the home. The Nigerian native also asked the courts to bar Porsha from filming The Real Housewives of Atlanta in their marital home since she has a nearly $1.8 million home she still owns. He also demanded Porsha and Bravo to turn over any documents, photographs and unreleased footage they have filmed for the upcoming season in his home since February 2024 and a copy of his ex’s contract.

In a May filing from Porsha, who is currently worth less than $500k now, she asked the court to make Simon pay her $50k for attorney’s fees and litigation expenses. But Simon’s lawyer argued that his ex’s demands were against Georgia law. The Real Housewives of Atlanta began filming season 16 in May 2024 with a few new cast members, but with the former couple’s court battles, Porsha is not allowed to film inside her and Simon’s home. Porsha filed an emergency order on May 20 to allow her to film her day-to-day life in the home, since it is vital to her ability to sustain her income and employment.
